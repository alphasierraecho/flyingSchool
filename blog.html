BLOGS BY OUR FLYERS

Learn the aviation fundamentals in fun way

The four forces making up the principle of flight are lift, weight, drag, and thrust. The forces all interact together to determine an airplane's trajectory. Lift and weight are opposing forces, as are thrust and drag. All are equally important, and they must be balanced to maintain level flight.

Best office in the world

The cockpit, a sanctuary nestled amidst the clouds, embodies the quintessence of freedom and responsibility. Within its confines, the world transforms into a canvas of infinite possibilities. Surrounded by an array of controls, screens, and instruments, the pilot assumes the role of conductor, orchestrating the symphony of flight with precision and grace. Through the panoramic windshield, the horizon unfolds in a mesmerizing panorama, offering a front-row seat to nature's grandeur. Here, amidst the hum of engines and the whisper of air, time seems to stand still, granting a rare sanctuary from the hustle and bustle of the world below. Every flight is a journey of discovery, a fusion of technological mastery and human intuition, where challenges are met with determination and triumphs celebrated with quiet satisfaction. In the cockpit, one finds not just a workplace, but a realm where dreams take flight and the boundaries of possibility are constantly pushed. Indeed, it is more than an office; it is a realm of endless wonder and unparalleled beauty.
